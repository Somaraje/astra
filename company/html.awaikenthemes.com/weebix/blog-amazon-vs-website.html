Selling on Amazon vs Your Own Website: Pros, Cons & What's Best

  • 20 February 2026
  • Marketplace
  • Asthra Digital Solutions Team

One of the most common questions e-commerce entrepreneurs face is whether to sell on Amazon, build their own website, or do both. Each approach has distinct advantages and drawbacks, and the right choice depends on your business model, budget, and long-term goals. Let's break down the pros and cons of each to help you make an informed decision.

The Pros of Selling on Amazon

Amazon is the world's largest online marketplace, with over 300 million active customer accounts. Listing your products on Amazon gives you instant access to this massive built-in audience. You don't need to worry about driving traffic — customers are already there, searching for products to buy. Amazon also handles fulfillment through FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon), taking care of storage, packing, shipping, and customer service.

The Cons of Selling on Amazon

The biggest downside of selling on Amazon is the fees. Between referral fees, FBA fees, and advertising costs, Amazon can take 30-40% of your revenue. You also have very limited control over your brand presentation — product pages look the same for every seller, and Amazon can change its policies at any time. Worst of all, you don't own your customer data. If Amazon suspends your account, you lose access to your entire business overnight.

The Pros of Your Own Website

With your own e-commerce website (built on platforms like WooCommerce or Shopify), you have complete control over your brand, design, customer experience, and pricing. You own all customer data, which allows you to build email lists, run retargeting campaigns, and create personalized shopping experiences. Profit margins are significantly higher since you're not paying marketplace fees, and you're building a valuable business asset that you fully control.

Selling on Amazon is like renting a shop in someone else's mall. Your own website is like owning the building — you control the rules, the experience, and the profits.

The Cons of Your Own Website

The main challenge with your own website is that you're responsible for driving all the traffic yourself. Unlike Amazon, there's no built-in audience waiting to discover your products. You'll need to invest in SEO, paid advertising, social media marketing, and content marketing to attract customers. There are also upfront costs for website design, development, hosting, and ongoing maintenance. However, these costs are typically far lower than Amazon's long-term fees.

The Best Strategy: Use Both

For most e-commerce businesses, the smartest strategy is to use both channels. Start selling on Amazon to leverage their audience and generate immediate revenue, while simultaneously building your own website as your long-term brand home. Use Amazon to acquire customers, then redirect them to your website for repeat purchases through email marketing and loyalty programs.

  • Amazon fees — referral fees (8-15%), FBA fees, storage fees, and advertising costs can consume 30-40% of your revenue.
  • Brand control — your own website gives you 100% control over branding, design, and customer experience.
  • Customer data — you own all customer data on your website, enabling email marketing and retargeting.
  • Long-term value — your own website is a business asset you can scale, while Amazon can change its rules anytime.
  • Multi-channel approach — using both Amazon and your own site maximizes reach while building brand equity.
